CrashLens – Crash Severity Prediction

AI & Transportation Safety • 2026

Project Overview

SHAP-driven crash severity prediction across 477,801 NHTSA crash records (2020–2023) using an ensemble of Random Forest, XGBoost, LightGBM, and FT-Transformer models. Achieves 44.9% balanced accuracy with 42.6% fatal crash sensitivity on a heavily imbalanced 5-class KABCO dataset. SHAP analysis pinpoints ejection status, restraint use, and vehicle age as the dominant risk factors — providing explainable, actionable insights for traffic safety engineers and policy makers.

WorkZoneSafe FL – Near-Miss Risk Detection

AI & Work Zone Safety • 2026

Project Overview

Connected vehicle BSM-based near-miss risk prediction system for Florida highway work zones. Trained on 43,200 CitySim trajectory windows with TTC/PET-derived conflict labels, an XGBoost classifier achieves AUC-ROC 0.847 — identifying dangerous vehicle interactions before they escalate. SHAP analysis reveals speed differential, following distance, and CMV presence as top risk drivers. CMVs account for 47% of fatal work zone crashes despite only 12% of traffic volume. The dashboard flags the top 5 highest-risk Tampa metro corridors to guide FDOT deployment decisions.

SignalGuard – Hurricane Traffic Signal Risk Intelligence

AI & Disaster Resilience • 2026

Project Overview

XGBoost model (AUC-ROC 0.916, 5-fold CV 0.898 ± 0.017) predicting hurricane-induced traffic signal outages across 11,186 intersections in 5 Gulf Coast cities — Houston, New Orleans, Miami, Jackson, and Charlotte. Integrates NOAA storm tracks, FEMA flood zones, LED adoption rates, and spatial criticality scoring to rank signal vulnerability. Five evidence-based power adaptation strategies (tiered mode scheduling, LED upgrades, green wave corridors, automated battery switching, demand-responsive cycling) extend backup runtime from 4–8 hours to 72+ hours — meeting FDOT's Category 3+ storm minimum with no additional hardware.
